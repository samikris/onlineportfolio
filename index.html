About Me

Hi! I’m Samiksha, a junior in DEDA. My goal this year is to learn more about engineering and its applications, especially in areas I am interested in such as aerospace and nuclear engineering. As computer science becomes increasingly important, I also hope to develop a set of skills that allows me to combine coding and physical aspects of engineering together. I think it is important not only to solve problems with my engineering knowledge, but also communicate it to others. So far, I have spent my time in DEDA finding what I am passionate about by experimenting with different areas of engineering. After a few field trips last year, I have also become interested in using engineering or computer science in research to tackle climate change and other environmental issues. This year, I have been exploring electrical engineering because I am in Digital Electronics, an engineering specialization course that focuses on building and understanding digital circuits.

Developing problem solving skills, verbal communication, and analytical thinking skills has become increasingly important to me. I have accumulated skills like knowledge about modern technology and coding and being observant of my surroundings. With this, I have been able to process challenging concepts with ease and apply them to aspects of my environment. In high school, I have the ability to learn and communicate concepts despite their challenges because I am willing to overcome difficulty and make mistakes. I am a youth volunteer for Lindsay Wildlife Experience and the AAUW (American Association of University Women), where I share my scientific knowledge with the public and learn about various environmental issues. To challenge myself, I joined Gael Force Robotics and I have been a member of the competition team 5327S since 2020, where I attend competitions and assist my team captain in designing a robotic system that performs various functions in a game. On my own time, I have also been working on a Deep Learning and Machine Learning Specialization to learn how to work with data, especially related to the environment and wildfires.

These activities have taught me how to communicate my observations to others and design a complex system with a team. I would love to apply my knowledge to environmental problems closer to home, like figuring out how to protect local ecosystems and studying extreme climates and wildfires. I hope that my curiosity, hard work, and experiences in DEDA will allow me to find what I’m interested in this year.
